# Sustainability and Cooling Credits

[← Back to English Keyword Connection Map](KEYWORD_CONNECTION_MAP.md)

---

## What is sustainability?

Sustainability means the long-term maintenance of environmental, social, and economic systems.

However, if natural cooling functions are weakened and heat continues to accumulate in cities, soils, forests, and oceans, sustainability itself cannot be maintained.

A society cannot remain sustainable if water cycles weaken, soils dry, forest evapotranspiration declines, and ocean circulation loses its cooling and absorption functions.

---

## Connection to Cooling Credits

Cooling Credits connect sustainability to measurable heat-load reduction.

They assign value to cooling actions such as urban cooling, soil restoration, forest regeneration, water-cycle recovery, and ocean-circulation support.

```text
Sustainability
↓
Recovery of natural cooling functions
↓
Measurable cooling actions
↓
Cooling Credits
```

---

## Why it matters

A sustainable future requires more than emissions reduction alone.

Carbon sinks, soil microbes, water cycles, forest evapotranspiration, and ocean circulation must also be restored.

Cooling Credits are a proposed framework for connecting those recovery actions to markets, investment, and implementation.
